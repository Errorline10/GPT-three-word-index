The idea in 30 seconds

A public place addressed by three words.

Simple for a person to remember. Flexible enough to hold a community, project, reference collection, or conversation.

01

Three-word destinations

Every page is identified by exactly three words, such as /model/rocket/design.

02

Modular content

Pages are assembled from independent content_block units for text, code, links, media, and files.

03

Human-scale friction

The platform explores browser-side work and delivery patterns that increase the cost of industrial-scale harvesting.

04

Community-supported visibility

Content remains visible through an experimental display-token system that decays through real attention.

Project principles

Open experimentation with clear boundaries.

Human-first

Optimize for people reading, sharing, and building communities—not for behavioral extraction.

Technically honest

No claim of perfect bot prevention, secrecy, or magical encryption.

Standard security

Experimental content friction stays separate from account, payment, and financial security.

Low-cost by design

The platform should remain viable as a hobby project and scale only when its economics support growth.

Funding and governance

Founder-led. Community-built. Transparently operated.

Three Word Index is currently a founder-funded, volunteer-supported open-source project. Contributions do not automatically create employment, equity, ownership, or revenue-sharing rights.

If the hosted project generates revenue, funds will first support infrastructure, security, compliance, moderation, and a responsible operating reserve. Contributor bounties, contracts, or funded maintainer work may follow when resources permit.

Frequently asked questions

Before you jump in.

Is this supposed to make bots impossible?

No. The goal is to increase the cost and inconvenience of bulk automated extraction while keeping normal human use practical.

Do I need to understand the entire architecture?

No. You can contribute documentation, design, tests, infrastructure, or take ownership of one small content-block mini-project.

Is this cryptocurrency?

No. The proposed coin and display-token systems are internal platform accounting mechanisms, not speculative cryptocurrency.

Are contributors paid?

Contributions are currently voluntary. Future funding may support bounties, contracts, grants, or maintainer work, but no payment or employment is promised.

Can students and newer developers contribute?

Yes. The project intends to maintain small issues, documentation tasks, testing work, and self-contained component projects suitable for different experience levels.
